Southeast Pathology

Reconstruction of User Interface & User Experience

Introduction

Southeast Pathology provides specialized services to pathology instrumentation in hospitals and independent pathology laboratories throughout the United States. Their website offers a complete showcase of merchandise and services which they offer. However, southeastpathology.com was old, not responsive and was not living up to its potential. In October of 2017, the head developer at Southeast Pathology offered me an opportunity to contribute to the reconstruction of southeastpathology.com. Without hesitation, I accepted the challenge.

(Home page of original site)

Initial Ideas

Users are scientist or lab assistants searching for a specific piece of equipment for their lab. Southeast Pathology did not provide a smooth way to find a desired piece of equipment. I rebuilt the Product pages to provide the user with more visuals and useful information sooner in their search. This decreases the amount of searching required and makes the experience more intuitive.

(The before and after of a products page)

Increase Inquiries

(Reconstructed Contact Forms)

In order to get users to submit inquiries, I developed multiple instances where a contact form is available for submission. For example, on pages with high user views, I added a contact form at the bottom of the page. Hoping users feel more inclined to submit their information, thus increasing the opportunity of a sale.

Final Concept

There are 80 different products offered from Southeast Pathology. To give users a fast, smooth transition between product pages, I used Angular Routing to provide new content for individual products while limiting code for static components such as the Navigation Bar and the Footer. I also redesigned the presentation and visual layout of merchendise offered for each Product page. These changes create an entirely different experience for the user and address the pain points associated with the old website.
